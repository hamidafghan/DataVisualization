EcoBenefits Project

Pesto Team (Nabizada, Afghan and Ismail)

The final goal of this project is to create visualizations that highlight the ecological advantages provided by trees in Trento. This involves performing calculations and linking the "price" of trees to these advantages. By presenting the data in a visually appealing and understandable way, the website aims to communicate the importance and benefits of trees in the municipality of Trento.

To achieve this, the PESTO! group has utilized various techniques of data visualization, leveraging the data specifically focused on trees' abundance and position in Trento. The dataset itself consists of JSON and GeoJSON files, containing information about different tree attributes such as species, height, circumference, crown breadth, and geographic coordinates.

By utilizing HTML, CSS, and the D3.js library, the group has created charts and visualizations that present the data in an accessible manner. The website provides subpages dedicated to different assignments, each showcasing different visualizations and techniques to convey conclusions drawn from the Trento tree dataset.

Data visualization for trento tree dataset

For the initial task, we need to analyze and compare the abundance and distribution of different tree species across various "circoscrizioni" (neighborhoods) where the trees are located. Our approach involved creating five distinct charts:

The first chart presents a straightforward barchart displaying the top 20 tree species based on their abundance, along with additional information. The second chart employs a stacked barchart to compare the relationship between neighborhoods and the top 5 tree species.

Similar to the previous chart, the third one serves the same purpose but adopts small multiple charts, each representing a specific tree species. The fourth chart is a modified version of the second one, displaying a stacked bar chart that showcases the percentage distribution of tree species.

Lastly, the fifth chart utilizes waffle charts to illustrate the percentage distribution of tree species across neighborhoods.

For the second assignment, we need to visualize the distribution of four size measures for trees: height, crown width, canopy cover, and crown height. We can choose the measure using a dropdown menu. The five charts we created are:

The first chart, the histogram, visualizes the distribution of size measures for all tree species.The second chart, the box plot, provides an overview of the distribution of size measures, highlighting outliers and density.

The third chart, the correlation plot, demonstrates the relationship between CO2 production and size measures specifically for the top 5 tree types.

The fourth chart, the small multiples, shows individual correlations between CO2 production and size measures for each tree type using scatter plots.

Lastly, the fifth chart, the bubble chart, illustrates the correlation between size measures, CO2 production, and canopy size for the top 6 tree types, with bubble size indicating canopy size.

The objective of the third assignment in the Data Visualization course is to visually depict the distribution of trees within the geographical map of Trento's city. The city is divided into circoscrizioni, and the assignment aims to showcase this distribution using various measures.

The first map illustrates the abundance of trees in each circoscrizione. The second map depicts the density distribution of trees by considering the total area covered by trees in relation to the area of each circoscrizione.

Similar to the previous map, the third one focuses on tree density, but this time using the measure of oxygen production.

The fourth map provides a geographical representation of the locations of all the trees in Trento, using dots.

The final map highlights the top 10 trees in terms of quantity and shows their specific locations.

Assignment 3 →

The fourth assignment in Data Visualization focuses on analyzing temporal data. Specifically, we have collected daily temperature data for the city of Salorno from 1981 to 2021. The initial visualization showcases the monthly temperature trend for a given year. It includes separate line charts for maximum and minimum monthly temperatures, while the average monthly temperature is displayed as a scatterplot positioned between the two lines.

The second visualization features a radar chart that illustrates the maximum, minimum, and average monthly temperatures for the selected year, represented by three distinct lines.

Lastly, the final visualization employs a unique chart type called ridgeline to depict the monthly distribution of temperatures (both maximum and minimum) for the chosen year.

The objective of the final assignment in Data Visualization, Assignment 5, is to demonstrate the correlation between various metrics for specific types of trees. To achieve this, we utilized a specific type of flow chart called an alluvial (or Sankey) diagram. The sole diagram in this assignment aims to establish a relationship between the carbon storage, measured in kilograms, of the top ten tree species based on abundance, and the corresponding carbon storage values measured in euros.
